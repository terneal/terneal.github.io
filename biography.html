Let's meet my Personal Hero:

Linus Torvalds

Linus Benedict Torvalds, born December 28, 1969 in Helsinki, Finland. The Finnish-American programmer is a firm believer in open source software and user freedom, Torvalds created GIT and the Linux Operating System Kernel which is used by many Linux Distros and Android today. Torvalds developed Linux 1.0 on March 14, 1994 with prototypes being available publicly in late 1991. GIT was created by Torvalds in April of 2005 as an open source project manager, but maintenance was handed off to Junio Hamano in July of that year. Torvalds has received many awards over his career, from the Lovelace Medal to having two asteroids named after him.
